About

The webpages contain unique visualization dashboard for the research data at Center for Employee Health Studies (CEHS) located at UIC. This data includes workers’ compensation claims from a Third Party Administrator (TPA) for self-insured companies. Specifically, researchers are looking at the costs of workers’ compensation claims for Illinois and 6 other Midwestern states, and stratifying by whether the workers’ compensation claim was litigated and which body part was injured. This will help them to understand the cost differentials between Illinois and the other states, as well as by injury type within Illinois. Costs being considered include lost work time, medical costs, and administrative/legal costs.

How do I use the site

Each of the tab contains their corresponding representation of the data.
    1. The fisrt tab "Tabular Display" shows the data in a tabular form when you fill out the required fields.
    2. The second tab "State Map" shows the workers' compensation data for the six mid-west states along with its tabular content.
    3. The third tab "Cost Differential" shows the data in a scatter-plot graph with the comparison of the mean of IL with other states.

What are the functions of the tab?

Every section of the tab provide the data about workers compensation, and the corresponding costs incurred

How do I filter the details in "Tabular Display" tab?

Please follow the following instructions (Make sure that you specify all the filters):
    1. Specify the part of the body injured (E.g, upper extremities, Lower Extremities, trunk, head)
    2. Click any one of the options for the cases (Ligitaged cases or Non-litigated cases or all the categories) content.
    3. Pick any of the state from the dropdown menu
    4. Specify any year from the dropdown list for which you need to view the cases
    5. Click "Submit" and you will be given the list of cases matching your filter criteria

What does the "State Map" show?

    1. A mid-west map is shown which is color coded. The most darkest state is the one with highest medical cost and the most lightest state is the one least medical cost.
    2. Also, when place your mouse pointer on any of the states, it shows the total cases, litigated cases, non-litigated cases, total indemnity cost, total medical cost and total expenses cost associated with the state.
    3. When you click the option to "Show table" the above information is showed in table with the first record having the least medical cost.

What does the "Cost Differential" show?

    1. The red line above the X-axis and Y-axis show the mean for Illinois for respective chosen costs.
    2. The points on the graph show the cost for each of the state. Each state is denoted by a unique shape.
    3. When you hover over the points, the data corresponding to the point is displayed.
    4. You can further filter the data by choosing filter options and selecting and deselecting the states.

What does the timeline chart show?

    1. The timeline chart shows the cost incurred for Litigated and Non-Litigated cases for Illinos over the years
    2. When you hover over the graph lines, a tooltip with the cost for that particular time period appears
    3. You can further zoom in and zoom out of the timeline to view accurate data.

What does the Box chart show?

    1. The red line shows the average cost of Illinois
    2. The boxes corresponding to each of the state shows the minimum cost, cost at the lowest quartile, median, cost at the upper quartile, highest cost incured
    3. When you hover over the boxes, each of the cost is displayed
